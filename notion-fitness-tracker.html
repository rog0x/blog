Track Your Workouts in Notion: Better Than Fitness Apps

March 20, 2026 · 5 min read · NotionFitnessHealth

Strong costs $5/month. MyFitnessPal costs $20/month. Strava costs $12/month. That's $444/year for three apps that don't talk to each other.

Or you could use Notion — which you probably already pay for — and track everything in one connected workspace. Here's why it's better.

The Problem with Fitness Apps

  • Subscription fatigue. Another $5-20/month. Another app. Another login.
  • Walled gardens. Your workout data is stuck in Strong. Your nutrition is stuck in MyFitnessPal. They don't connect.
  • Rigid formats. The app decides how you track. If your workflow doesn't match their design, too bad.
  • Data export limitations. Try getting your 3 years of workout data out of most fitness apps. Good luck.

Why Notion Works

  • One workspace. Workouts, nutrition, habits, sleep, goals — all connected.
  • You own your data. It's in your Notion account. Export anytime.
  • Fully customizable. Track what YOU care about. Add fields, remove fields, change views.
  • Connected to everything else. Your fitness goals connect to your daily planner, your habits connect to your journal.
  • No extra cost. If you already use Notion, it's free.

What a Notion Fitness Setup Looks Like

Workout Log

Every workout: date, type (push/pull/legs/cardio), exercises performed, sets/reps/weight, duration, how you felt. Calendar view shows your training frequency. Table view lets you filter by exercise to see progression.

Exercise Library

Your personal database of exercises: muscle group, equipment needed, difficulty, instructions. Link to your workout log so you can see "last time I did bench press, I did 4x8@80kg."

Nutrition Tracker

Simple daily logging: meal name, calories, protein, carbs, fat. Not as detailed as MyFitnessPal's food database, but enough for macro tracking. Most people don't need barcode scanning — they eat the same 20 meals.

Habit Tracker

Daily checkboxes: water, vitamins, stretching, meditation, steps. A formula counts your streak. Seeing an unbroken chain of checkmarks is surprisingly motivating.

Body Metrics

Weekly weigh-ins, measurements, progress photos. A simple table that shows your trend over months. No AI analysis needed — just see the numbers go in the right direction.

The Power of Connection

Here's what no fitness app gives you: your workout log links to your habit tracker links to your sleep log. You can answer questions like:

  • "Do I lift more on days I sleep 8+ hours?"
  • "Does my mood correlate with workout frequency?"
  • "Which weeks did I hit all my habits?"

Connected databases turn individual data points into patterns.

Getting Started

  1. Start with the workout log. Just log what you do. Date, exercises, sets/reps. That's it.
  2. Add habits after 1 week. Pick 3-5 daily habits. Check them off. Build the streak.
  3. Add nutrition when ready. Don't track calories on day one. Get the workout habit first.
  4. Review weekly. Every Sunday: what went well, what to adjust. 5 minutes.

The best fitness tracker is the one you actually use. If you're already in Notion every day, your fitness tracker should be there too.
