Weekly Meal Prep in Notion

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionMeal PrepHealth

Americans spend $3,500/year on dining out. Meal prep cuts that by 60-70% and takes 2-3 hours on Sunday. The hardest part isn't cooking — it's deciding what to make. Notion solves the planning problem.

The Sunday System

  1. Saturday evening: pick 4-5 recipes from your database for the week
  2. Notion auto-generates the grocery list from recipe ingredients
  3. Sunday morning: shop from the list (30-45 min)
  4. Sunday afternoon: batch cook (2-3 hours)
  5. Monday-Friday: grab containers, heat, eat

The key insight: you don't need 21 different meals. 4-5 recipes produce lunch and dinner for a week. Breakfast can be 2 options on rotation. Variety comes from rotating recipes week to week, not day to day.

Building Your Recipe Database

Start with 20 recipes you actually like. For each one, track: ingredients with quantities, prep time, cook time, servings, calories/macros, and a "meal prep friendly" rating. Some dishes are great fresh but terrible reheated — avoid those.

  • Proteins — chicken thighs, ground turkey, salmon, tofu, eggs. Pick 2-3 per week.
  • Carbs — rice, sweet potatoes, pasta, quinoa. Cook in bulk, portion out.
  • Vegetables — roasted broccoli, sautéed spinach, raw salad. Add variety here.
  • Sauces — this is how you make the same chicken taste different 5 days in a row. Teriyaki, pesto, buffalo, curry, chimichurri.

Auto Grocery Lists

The killer feature: select this week's recipes, and Notion generates a combined grocery list with quantities. If two recipes both need onions, it sums them. No more buying duplicates or forgetting ingredients.

Macro Tracking

If you're tracking calories or macros, Notion calculates per-meal nutrition from recipe data. See your weekly average protein, carbs, fat without logging every bite in MyFitnessPal. Because you prepped it all, you know exactly what's in each container.
