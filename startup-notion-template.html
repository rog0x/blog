Run Your Startup from Notion: Replace 10 SaaS Tools

March 20, 2026 · 7 min read · StartupNotionFounder

Early-stage startups don't need Jira, Salesforce, Lattice, Linear, and 6 other tools. They need one workspace where everything connects. Notion is that workspace.

Here's the system that replaces 10 tools with 12 connected pages.

What You're Replacing

  • Jira → Sprint Planner page with kanban and story points
  • Salesforce → Customer CRM with pipeline stages
  • Lattice → OKR Tracker with quarterly objectives and key results
  • Linear → Product Roadmap with impact/effort scoring
  • Carta → Investor Pipeline with check sizes and stages
  • Greenhouse → Hiring Pipeline with candidate tracking
  • ChartMogul → Metrics Dashboard with MRR, churn, LTV
  • Google Slides → Pitch Deck Builder with slide management
  • Confluence → Knowledge Base with processes and docs
  • Notion itself → Meeting Notes with action items

Monthly cost of those 10 tools: $500-2000. Monthly cost of Notion: $10 per seat.

The 12-Page System

1. Dashboard

Your company at a glance: MRR, active users, runway, burn rate. Embedded views from every other page. Check it once in the morning, know everything.

2. OKR Tracker

Quarterly objectives linked to measurable key results. Progress bars show how you're tracking. Weekly check-ins keep everyone aligned.

3. Product Roadmap

Features scored by impact and effort. Kanban columns: Now, Next, Later, Done. Every feature links to the OKR it serves.

4. Customer CRM

Every lead and customer. Pipeline stages, lifetime value, last contact, notes. Not as powerful as Salesforce, but 10x easier to maintain at your stage.

5. Investor Pipeline

Fundraising tracker: investor name, check size, stage (Research → Intro → Meeting → DD → Committed), last contact, notes. See your entire round at a glance.

6. Meeting Notes

Every meeting generates action items. Action items link to team members. Nothing falls through the cracks.

7. Pitch Deck Builder

Manage your deck slide by slide. Track which version each investor saw. Notes per slide for what to emphasize.

8. Hiring Pipeline

Open roles with job descriptions. Candidates with stage tracking (Applied → Phone → Technical → Onsite → Offer). Interview notes linked to candidates.

9. Competitive Analysis

Feature comparison matrix. Know exactly where you're ahead and where you're behind. Updated when competitors ship.

10. Metrics Dashboard

Monthly snapshot: MRR, churn, CAC, LTV, DAU/MAU. Track growth over time. Know your numbers cold for investor conversations.

11. Sprint Planner

2-week sprints with tasks, assignees, story points. Velocity tracking so you can predict delivery dates.

12. Knowledge Base

Processes, playbooks, onboarding docs. Write it once, everyone references it. Reduces "how do I..." questions by 80%.

When to Upgrade

This system works for teams of 1-15 people. When you need to upgrade:

  • CRM: When you have 500+ contacts and need automation → HubSpot
  • Sprints: When you have 10+ engineers and need custom workflows → Linear
  • Metrics: When you need real-time dashboards → Mixpanel + Metabase
  • Hiring: When you're hiring 5+ roles simultaneously → Ashby

Until then, Notion handles it all. And by the time you need specialized tools, you'll have the revenue to afford them.
