Build a Reading Habit with Notion

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionReadingBooks

You want to read more. Everyone does. The problem isn't motivation — it's visibility. When you can see your reading streak, your annual progress, and your book ratings, reading becomes a game you want to win.

The System

  1. Set an annual goal. 12 books/year = 1/month. 24 = 2/month. Start realistic.
  2. Log every book. Title, author, genre, start/finish date, rating. Gallery view with covers makes your library beautiful.
  3. Track daily. A 2-second entry: which book, how many pages. Over time, you see patterns — when you read most, which genres you favor.
  4. Take notes. Key takeaways, favorite quotes, would-recommend-to. A year later, you remember what you learned instead of just a vague "it was good."
  5. Maintain a wishlist. When someone recommends a book, add it. When you finish one, pick the next from your pre-curated list.

Why Not Goodreads

  • Goodreads hasn't been updated since Amazon bought it
  • The social features are noise — you want focus, not feeds
  • Your notes and highlights live somewhere else (Kindle, Notes app)
  • Notion keeps everything in one place: the book, the notes, the reading log

Making It Stick

The secret to reading more: make it the default, not an exception.

  • Read 20 pages before opening your phone in the morning
  • Replace 30 minutes of evening screen time with a book
  • Keep your Kindle/book visible — not in a drawer
  • Log your reading in Notion before bed — seeing the streak motivates tomorrow
