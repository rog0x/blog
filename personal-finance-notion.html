Track Your Money in Notion: The Only Budget Template You Need

March 20, 2026 · 5 min read · NotionFinanceBudget

Mint shut down. YNAB costs $99/year. Spreadsheets are boring. But you already have Notion open all day.

Here's how to turn it into your personal finance dashboard — and why it works better than dedicated finance apps.

Why Notion for Money

  • No subscription. You already pay for Notion (or use it free). No extra cost.
  • Your data, your rules. No selling your transaction data to advertisers.
  • Connected to everything. Your budget links to your goals. Your subscriptions link to your expense tracker.
  • Works everywhere. Phone, tablet, laptop. Same data.

The 8-Page System

1. Dashboard

Your financial snapshot: this month's income vs expenses, savings rate, net worth, upcoming bills. Check it in 10 seconds every morning.

2. Income Tracker

Every income source: salary, freelance, investments, side projects. Categorized and dated. Know exactly where your money comes from.

3. Expense Tracker

Log every expense. Category, amount, date, payment method. The "essential" checkbox separates needs from wants. Monthly totals by category show where your money goes.

4. Budget Planner

Set monthly budgets per category. Formulas auto-calculate actual spend vs budget. Color coding shows what's over, under, or on track.

5. Subscription Manager

This one page pays for the entire template. List every subscription, see the total monthly cost, and find the ones you forgot about. Most people discover $20-50/month in forgotten subscriptions.

6. Savings Goals

Emergency fund, vacation, new laptop, down payment. Each goal has a target, deadline, and progress bar. Seeing the bar fill up is addictive in the best way.

7. Investment Portfolio

Basic tracking: what you own, how much it's worth, your return. Not a replacement for your brokerage — just a bird's-eye view of your total portfolio.

8. Net Worth Tracker

Monthly snapshot: total assets minus total liabilities. The number that actually matters. Watching it trend upward over months is the best motivation.

Getting Started

Don't try to track everything on day one. Start with:

  1. Week 1: Log expenses only. Just get into the habit of recording what you spend.
  2. Week 2: Add the subscription manager. Cancel what you don't use.
  3. Week 3: Set up budgets for your top 3 spending categories.
  4. Month 2: Add savings goals and income tracking.
  5. Month 3: Start tracking net worth monthly.
