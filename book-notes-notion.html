Build a Knowledge Base from Books in Notion

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionReadingKnowledge

You read 20 books a year but remember maybe 5% of what you read. The problem isn't reading — it's retention. A structured note-taking system in Notion turns books from entertainment into a searchable, connected knowledge base.

The Three-Layer System

  • Layer 1: Highlights — capture quotes and passages that resonate. Don't highlight everything — only things that surprise you or change how you think about something.
  • Layer 2: Your Thoughts — for every highlight, write 1-2 sentences in your own words. Why does this matter? How does it connect to what you already know? This is where learning happens.
  • Layer 3: Concepts — extract the big ideas and connect them across books. "Compound interest" isn't just finance — it shows up in relationships, skills, health. Link the concept to every book that discusses it.

Connecting Ideas Across Books

The real value emerges when your library grows past 20 books. You'll notice that "Deep Work" and "Flow" discuss the same concept from different angles. "Atomic Habits" and "The Compound Effect" reinforce each other. These connections are your unique insights — no one else has your exact combination of reading and experience.

The 30-Minute Book Review

  1. After finishing, let the book sit for 2-3 days
  2. Review your highlights (10 minutes)
  3. Write a 3-sentence summary: what's it about, key insight, who should read it (5 minutes)
  4. Extract 3-5 key concepts and link to existing concepts in your database (10 minutes)
  5. Rate the book and add to your "recommend to others" list if applicable (5 minutes)

This review step is what turns passive reading into active knowledge. Skip it, and the book fades within a month. Do it, and you'll reference those notes for years.
