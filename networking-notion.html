Track Professional Networking in Notion

March 20, 2026 · 3 min read · NotionNetworkingCareer

You meet someone at a conference, exchange cards, say "let's connect" — and never follow up. Three months later you can't remember their name. A networking tracker turns random encounters into lasting professional relationships.

What to Track

  • Contact details — name, company, role, email, LinkedIn, how you met, what you talked about
  • Conversation log — every interaction: coffee chat, email thread, conference encounter. Date, topic, key takeaways
  • Follow-up queue — who needs a follow-up, when, about what. The system that prevents "I should have reached out months ago"
  • Relationship strength — rate 1-5. Strong relationships get maintained quarterly, new ones need monthly touchpoints

The 48-Hour Rule

After meeting someone, follow up within 48 hours. Reference something specific from your conversation. "Great talking about the migration to microservices at your company — here's that article I mentioned." This one habit puts you in the top 5% of networkers because almost nobody does it.

Quarterly Touch-Base

Set a reminder to reach out to key contacts every quarter. Share something useful: an article, a job posting they'd know about, a congratulations on their promotion. Networking isn't asking for favors — it's staying visible and being genuinely helpful.
