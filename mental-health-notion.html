Track Mental Health in Notion

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionMental HealthWellness

Mental health apps collect your most personal data and store it on someone else's server. Notion keeps everything local to your workspace — private, searchable, and under your control. No subscription, no data harvesting.

The Daily Check-In

Takes 2 minutes. Every morning or evening, log:

  • Mood (1-10) — simple number. Over time, you'll see trends you can't spot in the moment.
  • Energy level — separate from mood. You can be happy but exhausted, or energetic but anxious.
  • Sleep quality — hours + quality rating. Sleep correlates with everything else.
  • Anxiety level (1-10) — track separately from mood. Identify triggers.
  • Gratitude — 3 things. Research consistently shows this improves wellbeing.
  • Wins — 1 thing that went well. On bad days, this matters most.

Identifying Triggers

After 2-3 weeks of data, patterns emerge. Maybe anxiety spikes every Sunday evening (work dread). Maybe mood drops after social media. Maybe sleep under 7 hours predicts bad days. You can't change what you can't see — tracking makes the invisible visible.

Therapy Session Notes

If you're in therapy, structured session notes are powerful. Before the session: what you want to discuss. During/after: key insights, homework, action items. Over months, you build a searchable record of your therapeutic journey. When your therapist asks "what did we talk about last time?" — you know.

Coping Strategy Library

Build a personal database of what works for you. For anxiety: box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, cold water on wrists. For low mood: walk outside, call a friend, cook something. Rate each strategy's effectiveness. When you're in a crisis, you don't have to think — just open the list.
