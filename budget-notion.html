Budget in Notion Using the 50/30/20 Rule

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionFinanceBudget

Most budgets fail because they're too complicated. The 50/30/20 rule fixes that: 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings. Three numbers. That's it. Notion makes it automatic.

The 50/30/20 Breakdown

  • 50% Needs — rent, utilities, groceries, insurance, minimum debt payments. Non-negotiable expenses you'd pay even if you lost your job tomorrow.
  • 30% Wants — dining out, subscriptions, hobbies, shopping, entertainment. Things you enjoy but could cut if needed.
  • 20% Savings — emergency fund, retirement, investments, extra debt payments. Future you will thank present you.

On $4,000/month after tax: $2,000 needs, $1,200 wants, $800 savings. If your needs exceed 50%, that's the first thing to fix — cheaper housing, refinance, cut insurance costs.

Setting It Up in Notion

  1. Create a Transactions database: Date, Amount, Category (relation), Type (Income/Expense)
  2. Create a Categories database: Name, Type (Need/Want/Saving), Monthly Limit
  3. Add a rollup formula to calculate spending per category
  4. Build a dashboard showing: total income, total by category, remaining budget per category
  5. Log expenses daily — takes 30 seconds per entry

The Daily Habit

The trick isn't the system — it's using it. Every evening, log what you spent. Two minutes. After a week, you'll see patterns. After a month, you'll know exactly where your money goes. Most people are shocked by dining out and subscriptions.

Savings Goals

Track specific goals: emergency fund ($10K), vacation ($2K), new laptop ($1.5K). Seeing progress bars fill up is motivating. Notion formulas can show percentage complete and estimated completion date based on your monthly savings rate.
