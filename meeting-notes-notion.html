Never Lose a Meeting Action Item Again

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionMeetingsProductivity

The average professional spends 31 hours per month in meetings. Most produce notes that nobody reads, action items that nobody tracks, and decisions that get re-debated a month later.

Fix that with one Notion system: meetings → action items → decisions. All linked.

The Problem with Meeting Notes

  • Written in a Google Doc that gets lost
  • Action items buried in paragraph text
  • No tracking of who does what by when
  • Decisions made but not recorded — re-debated later

The Solution

Meeting database — every meeting: date, type, attendees, agenda, notes. Calendar view shows the week. Table view lets you search.

Action items — linked to the meeting they came from. Owner, deadline, status. Board view shows what's to do, in progress, and done. Overdue filter shows what fell through the cracks.

Decision log — linked to the meeting. Context, options considered, what was decided, who decided. Six months later when someone asks "why did we choose Postgres?" — you have the answer.

Templates That Save Time

Pre-built templates for common meeting types:

  • Standup: Yesterday / Today / Blockers
  • 1:1: How are you / Progress / Challenges / Feedback / Actions
  • Team sync: Updates / Metrics / Blockers / Announcements
  • Retrospective: Went well / Improve / Actions
