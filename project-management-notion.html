Project Management in Notion: Sprints, Retros, and Team Tracking

March 20, 2026 · 6 min read · NotionProject ManagementAgile

Jira is powerful but overkill for teams under 15 people. Linear is fast but opinionated. Notion sits in the sweet spot: flexible enough for your workflow, structured enough to keep things organized.

Here's how to run software projects in Notion without losing your mind.

The 10-Page System

Sprint Board

Kanban columns: Backlog, In Progress, Review, Done. Each task has story points, assignee, priority, blocker flag. Calendar view shows what's due this sprint. Table view shows total points per person — instant capacity check.

Daily Standups

Template for each standup: Yesterday, Today, Blockers — per team member. Takes 30 seconds to fill in. Over time, becomes a searchable history of what the team worked on.

Retrospectives

After each sprint: What went well, What to improve, Action items with owners. Action items carry forward until resolved. After 6 months, you have a clear record of how the team improved.

Risk Register

Most teams don't track risks until it's too late. Probability (1-5) x Impact (1-5) = Risk Score. Top risks reviewed weekly. Mitigation plans assigned to owners. This alone prevents most project disasters.

Release Tracker

Every release: version, features included, deploy date, status, rollback plan. The rollback plan is the part everyone forgets until 3am when production is down.

Team Directory

Who's on the team, their skills, current capacity, which projects they're on. Prevents over-allocation — the #1 cause of missed deadlines.

Decision Log

"Why did we choose PostgreSQL over MongoDB?" Six months later, nobody remembers. The decision log captures: context, options considered, decision made, who decided, and outcome. Prevents re-debating settled decisions.

Resource Planner

Who's allocated to which project, what percentage. Highlights over-allocation in red. Makes it visual when someone is assigned to 3 projects at 50% each — obviously impossible.

Why Notion Over Jira

  • Setup time: Notion: 30 minutes. Jira: 3 days of configuration.
  • Cost: Notion: $10/seat. Jira: $8/seat + premium for good features.
  • Flexibility: Notion: change anything anytime. Jira: needs an admin.
  • Connected data: Sprint tasks link to risks link to releases link to decisions. In Jira, that's 4 different dashboards.

When to Switch to Jira/Linear

Notion works for teams up to ~15 engineers. Switch when:

  • You need custom automations (auto-assign, auto-transition)
  • You need deep GitHub/GitLab integration (auto-close issues from PRs)
  • You have 20+ engineers and need workflow enforcement
  • You need enterprise audit logs and compliance

Until then, Notion handles it all — and your team probably already has it.
