Time Blocking in Notion: The Daily Planner That Works

March 20, 2026 · 3 min read · NotionProductivityTime Management

To-do lists are where tasks go to multiply. You add 5, cross off 2, and tomorrow there are 8. Time blocking fixes this: every task gets a time slot, and when the slots are full, your day is planned. No more "I'll get to it later."

The 10-Minute Morning Plan

  1. Pick your top 3 priorities. Not 5, not 10. Three.
  2. Block 90-minute deep work sessions for priorities 1 and 2
  3. Schedule meetings and calls in their slots
  4. Add admin tasks (email, Slack) in 30-minute blocks
  5. Leave 30 minutes unscheduled for the unexpected

The key: if it's not on the calendar, it doesn't happen. Treat your time blocks like meetings with yourself — don't cancel them for "quick" requests.

Energy-Based Scheduling

Most people have 2-4 hours of peak cognitive energy per day. Don't waste them on email. Track your energy levels for a week, then schedule accordingly:

  • Peak energy (usually 9-11am) — deep work, coding, writing, problem-solving
  • Medium energy (1-3pm) — meetings, collaborative work, reviews
  • Low energy (3-5pm) — admin, email, planning tomorrow, routine tasks

The Evening Review

5 minutes before closing your laptop: What did you accomplish? What moved to tomorrow? What was the day's win? This closes the loop and makes tomorrow's planning easier. After a week, you'll spot patterns — which tasks always slip, which time blocks are too ambitious.
