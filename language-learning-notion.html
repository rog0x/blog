Learn a Language with Notion

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionLanguageLearning

Duolingo gives you streaks but not structure. Anki gives you flashcards but not grammar. Notion gives you a complete system — vocabulary, grammar, study log, and progress — all connected.

The System

Vocabulary database — every word with translation, example sentence, category, and review date. The formula calculates when to review based on spaced repetition: new words review in 1 day, then 3, then 7, then 30. You never review too early or too late.

Grammar notes — organized by topic and level (A1-C2). Each rule has explanation, examples, and exercises. Your personal grammar reference.

Study log — daily: how long, what you focused on, how many new words, confidence level. Over months, patterns emerge — you learn best from podcasts? From conversation? From reading?

Resources — every app, book, podcast, YouTube channel you use. Rated and organized. When you plateau, you know which resources to switch to.

Progress tracking — monthly milestones on the CEFR scale (A1→C2). Total words learned, hours studied, level progression. The motivation to keep going.

Why Not Just Duolingo?

  • Duolingo doesn't track grammar separately
  • You can't organize vocabulary by category
  • No study log to track what works for YOU
  • Progress is gamified points, not actual language level
  • You can't add your own words from conversations/movies

Use Duolingo AND Notion. Duo for daily practice, Notion for the bigger picture.
