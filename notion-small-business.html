Run Your Small Business From Notion

March 20, 2026 · 5 min read · NotionBusinessCRM

HubSpot CRM: free but complex. FreshBooks: $17/month. Asana: $10.99/seat. For a 1-5 person business, that's $500+/year in tools that don't talk to each other.

Notion replaces all of them with one connected workspace. Here's how.

The Small Business System

Client Database — every client with their full history: projects done, invoices sent, total lifetime value. When a client calls, you see everything in one page.

Project Tracker — active projects linked to clients. Budget, deadline, team, status. Board view shows your pipeline at a glance.

Invoice Manager — create invoices, track what's sent, what's paid, what's overdue. The overdue filter is your reminder system. No more "forgot to send the invoice" moments.

Revenue Dashboard — monthly income and expenses. Know your profit. Know your best clients. Know your seasonal patterns. Data you need for tax time, all in one place.

Marketing Pipeline — leads from first touch to closed deal. Track where leads come from (referral vs website vs social). Know your conversion rate. Follow up on time.

Why Not a "Real" CRM?

  • Salesforce is built for 500-person sales teams, not solo consultants
  • HubSpot's free tier is powerful but takes weeks to set up properly
  • Notion takes 30 minutes to customize and you're running
  • Your whole team already uses Notion — no new tool to learn

When to Upgrade

Notion handles 1-10 employees easily. Switch to dedicated tools when:

  • You need email automation (→ HubSpot)
  • You have 100+ active leads (→ Pipedrive)
  • You need automated accounting (→ QuickBooks)
  • You have 20+ employees (→ Monday.com + accounting software)
