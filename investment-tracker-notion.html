Track Your Investment Portfolio in Notion

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionInvestingFinance

Brokerage apps show your current portfolio but not the full picture. They don't track cost basis across brokers, show total dividend income over time, or help you plan rebalancing. Notion gives you a unified view of everything you own.

What to Track

  • Holdings — every position across all accounts: stocks, ETFs, bonds, crypto, real estate. Ticker, shares, average cost, current value, gain/loss.
  • Transactions — every buy, sell, and dividend. This is your audit trail and tax preparation helper.
  • Asset allocation — what percentage is stocks vs bonds vs crypto vs cash? Does it match your target allocation?
  • Dividends — track every dividend payment, calculate annual yield, see which holdings generate the most passive income.

The Cost Basis Problem

If you buy VOO at $400, then again at $420, your average cost is $410. Simple for one holding, but across 20 positions in 3 accounts with dividend reinvestment? Notion calculates it automatically from your transaction log. This matters for taxes — knowing your exact cost basis prevents overpaying on capital gains.

Rebalancing

Target: 60% stocks, 25% bonds, 10% crypto, 5% cash. After a stock rally, you're 70/20/8/2. Notion highlights the drift and calculates exactly how much to buy/sell in each asset class to get back to target. Rebalance quarterly — disciplined, not emotional.

Dividend Income Tracking

Passive income from dividends is motivating. Track every payment, see monthly and annual income, calculate yield on cost (not current yield). A holding bought at $50 paying $2/year is 4% yield on cost even if the stock is now $100. That context matters for hold/sell decisions.
