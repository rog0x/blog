Track Workouts in Notion: Sets, Reps, and PRs

March 20, 2026 · 4 min read · NotionFitnessWorkout

Fitness apps lock your data behind subscriptions and disappear when the startup dies. Notion gives you a workout tracker you own forever, customize completely, and access from any device.

The Exercise Library

Start by building your exercise database. Every exercise with muscle group, equipment needed, and difficulty. This becomes your reference for building workouts. 50-100 exercises covers most programs.

  • Compound lifts — squat, deadlift, bench press, overhead press, barbell row. The foundation.
  • Accessories — curls, lateral raises, tricep pushdowns. The detail work.
  • Bodyweight — pull-ups, dips, push-ups. No gym required.
  • Cardio — running, cycling, rowing. Track time and distance.

Workout Templates

Create templates for your program: Push/Pull/Legs, Upper/Lower, Full Body, or custom splits. Each template lists exercises, target sets/reps, and rest times. Start a workout by duplicating a template and filling in actual numbers.

Tracking What Matters

For each set: weight, reps, and RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion, 1-10). RPE helps you know if 185 lbs felt heavy (RPE 9) or easy (RPE 6). Over time, you want the same weight at lower RPE — that's progress.

Track total volume (sets x reps x weight) per muscle group per week. Progressive overload means this number trends up over months. Notion formulas calculate this automatically.

PR Tracking

Personal records are motivating. Track your best for key lifts: 1RM, 3RM, 5RM. Notion can highlight when you hit a new PR by comparing today's entries against your historical max. Seeing "NEW PR" next to a set is the best feeling in the gym.

Body Measurements

Weight alone lies. Track waist, chest, arms, and body fat percentage monthly. You might gain 5 lbs but lose 2 inches on your waist — that's muscle gain, not fat. The scale doesn't tell that story.
